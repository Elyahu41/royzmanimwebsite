Methods of Calculations

  • The Amudeh Hora'ah Calendar based outside Eretz Yisrael was put together by Rabbi Leeor Dahan. It relies on the opinion of Minchat Cohen to modify the calculations based on how far north/south one is away from Israel. This opinion is quoted by Rabbi David Yosef in Halacha Brurah (Chelek 15, Siman 261, halacha 13).
  • The Ohr Hachaim Calendar based in Eretz Yisrael was used by Hacham Ovadia himself. It uses elevation to account for the hill-based environment, but does not alter the timings based on how far north/south one is located away from Israel based on what Rabbi Shlomo Benizri claims to be a verbal instruction by Hacham Ovadia Yosef.

Time format

  • 24h: A full 24 hour representation, used mainly outside the United States. Does not recept itself well with a watch.
  • 12h: The day is split into two, with the first half being 12->1-11 with an AM label, while the inverse is true for PM. Typically used in American countries
