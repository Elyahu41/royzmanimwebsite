Shovavim Guidelines - הרב אליהו נתנלי

Shovavim is a Kabbalistic time period where שערי שמים are open to rectify פגמי הברית. Although the עיקר התיקון is through לימוד תורה, there are series' of תפילות and תיקונים one can do to compliment it, such as תיקון כללי (an important set of מזמורי תהילים and passages from the BIH's לשון חכמים).

One of the accepted תיקונים is to fast, which Rabbi Netaneli suggests to do at least three times.

  • These fasts should be on days where one isn't working (due to potential deterierations in work quality, effectively stealing from the employer), nor can one fast on Friday (due to קדושת שבת). For most people, this means one should fast on a Sunday.
  • If communities are doing it, they may accomodate by treating this like a תענית, through saying עננו in חזרת הש"ץ, reading of ויחל משה and ברכת כוהנים by מנחה (provided the כהן is also fasting), when done close to שקיעה.
  • To accept the fast, one would read רבון העולמים on the Mincha of the day before, during אלקי נצור (for Shabbat, the text can be found in the weekday Tefilah). Those who cannot fast should substitue through donating a meal's worth of money to צדקה per-day that they aren't fasting.

Shovavim 2024 starts from פרשת שמות and ends at פרשת תצוה.
