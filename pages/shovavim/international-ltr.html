Shovavim Guidelines - הרב אליהו נתנלי

Shovavim is a Kabbalistic time period where שערי שמים are open to rectify פגמי הברית through תפילה and תיקונים.
It starts from פרשת שמות and ends this year in פרשת תצוה.
  • One should fast at minimum three days of this period, preferably on a day that one isn't working (due to potential deterierations in work quality, effectively stealing from the employer) or Friday (which one cannot fast due to קדושת שבת). For most people, this means a Sunday fast is the most efficient. To make up for the days of the shovavim period that one wouldn't be fasting (84 days), one would donate a meal's worth of money to צדקה.
  • To accept the fast, one should read רבון העולמים on the Mincha of the day before (for Shabbat, the text can be found in the weekday Tefilah), but one can still fast even without saying it.
  • Kehilot with over six people fasting should treat this like a תענית with עננו in חזרת הש"ץ, reading of ויחל משה and ברכת כוהנים by מנחה (provided the כהן is also fasting), when done close to שקיעה.
  • One should read תיקון כללי, a very important set of מזמורי תהילים and passages from the BIH's לשון חכמים.
