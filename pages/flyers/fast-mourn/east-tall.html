Calculation Explanation

Halachic Dawn (which is when fasts start; S"A Orach Chaim 562) is measured as 72 seasonal minutes before sunrise (Halikhot Olam Vol. I pg. 106). Locations further north or south of Israel will need to factor standard deviation, turning the time pre-seasonal factoring to 80 minutes in NY (Halacha Berurah, intro. to Chapter 261, Halacha 13)

For the context of fasts, Halachic Nightfall is calculated as 20 seasonal minutes (with standard-deviation accounted for) after sunset. Although one can rely on the letter of the law that uses 13.5 minutes instead (Yabia Omer YD 7:41), the recommended (albeit not strictly necessary) practice is to be stringent and hold by 20 minutes to accommodate the Rambam's opinion on nightfall (Ḥazon Ovadia, Arba Ta'aniyot, pg. 12). One need not wait any longer than this time for any fast (with the exception of Yom Kippur), even in the USA.

Independent of the fast day is the quarter-yearly Tekufa-fast, which happens at the beginning of every Halachic season. According to Maran zt"l (Halikhot Olam Vol. VII pg. 183, based on נהר מצרים, דף עז ע”ב), we have a time-buffer of an hour around the time of the Tekufa itself, by adding 30 minutes prior and following. During this time, one should abstain from plain water (with other beverages being permitted) due to a concern for spiritual danger.
